"Sassy" Sarah VAUGHAN

Jazz vocalist Sarah Vaughan performed with big bands before becoming a solo artist. She is known for singing 'Send in the Clowns' and 'Broken-Hearted Melody'. Born on 27 March 1924, in Newark, New Jersey, Sarah Vaughan grew up with a love of music and performing. Winning a talent competition held at Harlem's Apollo Theater launched her singing career. She worked with bandleaders Earl Hines and Billy Eckstine before becoming a successful solo performer.

Dave Brubeck

Dave Brubeck was an American jazz pianist and composer known for his unconventional meters. This contributed to his huge appeal.Born in Concord, California, on 6 December 1920, Dave Brubeck worked as a jazz pianist before co-founding the Dave Brubeck Quartet in 1951. Some of his most popular compositions included 'Blue Rondo a la Turk' and the song 'Take Five' which was composed by Brubecks longtime partner, saxophonist Paul Desmond. 'Take Five' was the first jazz instrumental to sell more than a million copies.

John Coltrane

A revolutionary and groundbreaking jazz saxophonist, John Coltrane was born on 23 September 1926, in Hamlet, North Carolina. Coltrane had created a definite sound of his own. Part of it was defined by an ability to play several notes at once, creating what would be later dubbed as his "sheets of sound."
